## Launch Strategy

### Phase 1: Private Beta (Weeks 1-4)

**Goal:** Validate the core product loop with real users before any marketing spend.

**Audience:** 100-500 hand-picked users
- Personal network (friends, family with kids)
- 3-5 mom group leaders willing to test with their group
- 10-20 small business owners for tertiary segment testing

**What to validate:**
- Filter precision (>90% accuracy — users don't lose good photos)
- Film profile emotional response (do people love how their photos look?)
- Roll completion rate (>60% of users who start a roll reach 36)
- Free print redemption rate and satisfaction
- "Would you pay for this?" (>30% yes)

**Feedback channels:**
- In-app feedback button
- Weekly 15-minute user interviews (5-10 per week)
- PostHog analytics dashboard monitoring

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Weeks 5-12)

**Goal:** Grow to 1,000-3,000 users organically; validate viral mechanics and conversion.

**Tactics:**
1. **Free first roll as acquisition engine** — Every new user gets 36 free prints. The physical product in their hands is the marketing.
2. **Circle invites** — Every Circle created generates 5-15 invite links. Each invite is a potential new user.
3. **Word of mouth from prints** — Prints on refrigerators, counters, desks generate "Where did you get those?" conversations.
4. **Content seeding in 5-10 mom groups** — Share the product naturally (not as ads) in Facebook groups, WhatsApp groups, local parent communities.

**Key metric:** Viral coefficient (K-factor) — how many new users does each existing user bring in? Target: >0.3 (each user generates 0.3 new users through Circle invites and word of mouth).

### Phase 3: Public Launch (Week 12+)

**Goal:** Transition from organic to scalable growth. Begin paid acquisition. Target 10K-25K users by end of Year 1.

## Customer Acquisition Channels

### Channel 1: Free First Roll (Primary Acquisition)

**How it works:** Every new user who develops their first roll gets 36 4×6 prints free, shipped to their door, no credit card required.

**Why it's powerful:**
- Physical product overcomes digital skepticism ("Is this actually good?")
- Creates an emotional peak moment (opening the envelope of prints)
- Generates word-of-mouth (prints displayed in homes → conversations)
- CAC is $12-15 per user — competitive with digital-only acquisition ($15-30+)
- The "free" framing removes all signup friction

**Conversion funnel:**
```
See Roll → Enter email → Upload photos → Develop first roll → Receive free prints → Experience quality → Upgrade to Roll+
```

**Target conversion:** 15-20% of free print recipients convert to Roll+ within 90 days.

### Channel 2: Circle Viral Loop (Organic Growth)

**How it works:** Roll+ subscribers create Circles and invite family/friends. Invitees join (free), see the curated feed, get inspired to develop their own rolls.

**Viral mechanics:**
1. User creates "Family Photos" Circle
2. Invites 5-15 family members via link
3. Invitees join, view the shared feed
4. Invitees are impressed by film-processed photos
5. Invitees upload their own photos, develop rolls
6. Some invitees upgrade to Roll+ to create their own Circles

**Target viral coefficient:** 0.3-0.5 (each user generates 0.3-0.5 new users)
- At K=0.3: 1,000 users → 1,430 total (organic amplification)
- At K=0.5: 1,000 users → 2,000 total

**Critical insight:** The Circle invite to grandparents, aunts, and uncles is the most powerful viral channel. Family members feel **obligated** to join when invited to see photos of grandchildren.

### Channel 3: Mom Group Marketing (Targeted Organic)

**How it works:** Seed authentic presence in online communities where the target audience gathers.

**Channels:**
- Facebook mom groups (local and national — "New Mom Support," "Moms of [City]," etc.)
- WhatsApp parent groups (school, daycare, activity-based)
- Reddit (r/Parenting, r/Mommit, r/NewParents, r/BabyBumps)
- Nextdoor (neighborhood parent communities)
- Mom influencer networks (micro-influencers, 5K-50K followers)

**Approach — authentic, not spammy:**
- Share genuine experiences: "I finally did something with the 20,000 photos on my phone"
- Show before/after (phone camera roll mess → beautiful film-processed prints)
- Lead with the free first roll offer
- Let the product quality do the selling

**Cost:** Low (time investment, not ad spend). Occasional gifted Roll+ subscriptions to group organizers.

### Channel 4: Instagram / TikTok Content (Brand Awareness)

**How it works:** Create content showcasing the film aesthetic and the emotional moment of receiving prints.

**Content types:**
1. **Before/after transformations** — Phone screenshot of camera roll → developed roll with Warmth film stock
2. **"Unboxing" prints** — Opening the kraft envelope, fanning through 4×6 prints
3. **Side-by-side film profiles** — Same photo in all 6 profiles, showing the range
4. **"20,000 photos, zero on your wall"** — Problem awareness content
5. **Parent-specific content** — "I finally printed my kids' photos" emotional narrative

**Platform strategy:**
- Instagram: Photo-focused content, Stories showcasing film profiles
- TikTok: Short unboxing videos, before/after reveals, "things I wish I knew" parent content
- Pinterest: Photo display inspiration, film aesthetic boards

**Cost:** Low initially (organic posting). Paid amplification at $500-$2,000/month for best-performing content.

### Channel 5: Paid Social Ads (Scaled Acquisition)

**When to start:** After organic channels validate messaging and conversion rates (Month 3-6).

**Target channels:**
- Facebook/Instagram Ads (primary — best targeting for parents)
- TikTok Ads (secondary — lower CPM, higher volume)
- Google Search (tertiary — capture intent: "print phone photos," "photo books for parents")

**Target CAC by channel:**
| Channel | Target CAC | Expected Quality |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Facebook/Instagram | $8-$15 | High (precise targeting) |
| TikTok | $5-$10 | Medium (volume, lower intent) |
| Google Search | $10-$20 | Very High (intent-based) |
| Organic / viral | $0-$3 | Highest (word of mouth) |

**Blended target CAC:** $8-$12 (weighted by channel mix)

**Ad creative strategy:**
- Lead with the free first roll offer ("Get 36 free prints")
- Show the film aesthetic (before/after images)
- Use parent-specific messaging ("Your kids deserve more than a camera roll")
- Never say "AI" — use "professional color correction" and "film stock"
- CTA: "Get your free prints" → landing page → email signup

### Channel 6: Referral Program (Planned)

**How it works:** Existing users refer friends. Both referrer and referee get rewards.

**Proposed structure:**
- **Referrer gets:** Free print order (36 prints) for every 3 successful referrals
- **Referee gets:** Free first roll (already standard) + 1 bonus film profile unlock
- **Tracking:** Unique referral links with UTM parameters

**Target:** Each active user refers 1-2 people over their lifetime.

**Potential partners:**

## Retention Strategy

### Why People Stay (Retention Drivers)

1. **Film profiles create exploration desire** — "What would my photos look like in Classic?" drives return visits
2. **Circle creates social obligation** — "My mom is waiting to see new photos" drives regular posting
3. **36-frame constraint creates completion satisfaction** — The mechanic of filling a roll is addictive
4. **Monthly print subscriptions create habit** — Physical arrival = physical reminder to keep using Roll
5. **Cloud backup creates lock-in** — "All my photos are here" makes switching costly
6. **Favorites portfolio grows over time** — Users accumulate a curated collection they don't want to lose

### Retention Tactics

| Tactic | Trigger | Goal |
|--------|---------|------|
| "Your roll is developed" push/email | Roll processing complete | Drive immediate return to app |
| "Your prints are on the way" email | Order shipped | Build anticipation |
| Monthly "Your memories are safe" email | 1st of month | Reinforce backup value |
| "You have 47 new photos to review" | Upload batch processed | Drive feed engagement |
| "3 photos away from a full roll" nudge | Roll at 33/36 | Drive completion |
| "You haven't developed a roll in 30 days" | Inactivity | Win back lapsed users |
| "Your January highlights" | Month end | Drive Favorites review |
| Circle activity notifications | Someone shares to your circle | Social engagement |
| "Book completion" nudges | 3, 7, 30 days after starting a book | Drive print orders |

### Churn Prevention

**Warning signs (tracked in analytics):**
- No login in 14+ days
- No checkmarks in 30+ days
- No roll developed in 60+ days
- Subscription downgrade inquiry

**Intervention sequence:**
1. Day 14: Push notification — "You have [N] new photos to review"
2. Day 21: Email — "[N] favorites waiting to be printed"
3. Day 30: Email — "We miss you" + 1 free additional film profile unlock
4. Day 45: Email — "Your photos are safe, but they'd look even better printed"
5. Day 60: Final email — Discount offer (1 month free Roll+)

## Positioning Against Alternatives

### vs. "Just use Google Photos"
"Google stores your photos. Roll develops them. Google gives you a digital archive. Roll gives you prints on your wall, a book on your shelf, and a private feed with your family. And we never train AI on your children's photos."

### vs. "Just use Instagram"
"Instagram shows you ads between photos of people you've never met. Roll shows you your own photos, made beautiful, shared only with the people you choose. No algorithm. No reels from strangers. Just your family's best moments."

### vs. "I'll get around to printing someday"
"You've been saying that for 5 years. You have 20,000 photos and zero on your wall. Roll makes it happen automatically. Pick your favorites, choose a look, and we print and ship them. Your first roll is free."

### vs. "Shutterfly / Chatbooks"
"Shutterfly gives you a blank canvas and hopes you have time to design. Roll starts with your best photos — already filtered, already color-corrected, already beautiful. The design is done. You just approve it."
